Welcome to the World of SAP

SAP is the abbreviation for Systems, Applications and Products. SAP software can manage multiple aspects of a business, including finances, operations, facilities and human resources. The enterprise resource planning (ERP) software is a critical feature of SAP software. It allows for integrating and analysing data from other areas, such as business intelligence and customer relationship management.

  • SAP Financial Accounting (FI)
  • SAP Financial Supply Chain Management (FSCM)
  • SAP Controlling (CO)
  • SAP Materials Management (MM)
  • SAP Sales and Distribution (SD)

  • SAP Project System (PS)

Levels of SAP Course Certification

Associate Certification

This is the fundamental level of certification, covering the basic requirements for an SAP professional. Suitable for those looking to start their journey in SAP.

Delta Certification

Builds upon the associate-level credentials with quarterly updates. Focuses on the newest features and enhancements.

Specialist Certification

Targets specific topics or components of SAP, offering narrow and specialized skills. Requires prior completion of the associate exam.

Professional Certification

The most advanced level, demanding actual project experience and a deep understanding of SAP solutions. Aims to significantly advance your career.

SAP Course Duration and Eligibility

Course duration varies but typically takes about 60 days. Eligibility includes a bachelor's or master's degree, with prior computer experience and sector knowledge beneficial.

SAP Sales & Distribution (SD)

SAP Sales and Distribution (SD) is an important module of SAP ERP consisting of business processes required in selling, shipping, billing of a product. The module is tightly integrated with other SAP modules like MM, FICO, and PP. Key sub-modules of SAP SD include Customer and Vendor Master Data, Sales, Delivery, Billing, Pricing and Credit Management, availability check, STO, and more.
